TASK

Generate formal caption candidates describing the video.

EVALUATION PRIORITIES

Your caption will be judged on:

1. Factual accuracy
   - Every claim must be supported by the vision output.
   - Do not add identities, locations, emotions, intentions, relationships, occupations, or events unless explicitly supported.

2. Specific visual grounding
   - Include 2-4 distinctive facts that make this video recognizable.
   - Prefer concrete subjects, actions, objects, colors, and environmental details.
   - Avoid generic captions that could describe many unrelated videos.

3. Formal style match
   - Use a clear, professional, objective, factual tone.
   - Sound like polished documentary narration or a high-quality stock-footage description.
   - Do not use humor, sarcasm, slang, exaggeration, or personal opinion.

4. Concision
   - Describe the central scene rather than every observation.
   - Use one natural sentence, preferably 18-35 words.

EVIDENCE BOUNDARY

- Prefer two or three unmistakable scene facts over a complete inventory.
- Do not add exact counts, durations, percentages, readable sign text, brands, species, materials, landmarks, or locations unless `caption_facts` explicitly supports them.
- Do not upgrade uncertain observations into confident claims.
- Camera movement is secondary. Mention it only when it is essential to what changes in the scene.
- Write the sentence directly. Never prefix it with a category, heading, or label.

GROUNDING PROCEDURE

Before writing, silently:

- identify the primary subject or scene;
- identify the main visible action;
- select the most distinctive environmental or visual details;
- remove uncertain or unsupported information;
- verify that the sentence sounds formal rather than conversational.

GENERATION RULES

- Treat `caption_facts` and high-confidence observations as the strongest evidence.
- Use temporal details only when they are important to understanding the video.
- Use gender-neutral language unless gender is explicitly and confidently supported.
- Do not identify a precise real-world location unless the vision output clearly establishes it.
- Do not mention the camera, frame, footage, or video unless doing so improves naturalness.
- Do not start every caption with the same phrase.
- Vary sentence structure naturally across different videos.
- Never copy a template mechanically.
