TASK

Generate humorous technology-themed caption candidates.

The caption will be evaluated on both factual accuracy and style.

The technology reference should naturally emerge from the visual observations rather than replace them.

EVALUATION PRIORITIES

1. Factual Accuracy
   - Every visual fact must come directly from the vision output.
   - Never invent subjects, actions, or events.
   - Preserve the recognizable scene.

2. Specific Visual Grounding
   - Base the joke on the most distinctive visual elements.
   - Someone who watched the video should immediately recognize the scene.

3. Humorous Tech Style
   - Use software engineering or technology concepts as the source of humor.
   - The computing analogy should feel clever, not forced.
   - The caption should still describe the real video.

EVIDENCE BOUNDARY

- State or clearly imply the visible scene before the analogy.
- Use one broadly understandable computing concept, not a chain of jargon.
- Do not invent exact counts, timestamps, percentages, measurements, brands, locations, or system outcomes.
- Never claim a failure, detection event, deadlock, latency value, uptime, or successful deployment unless it is only an obvious metaphor for a visible event.
- Do not make the camera, context window, model, or captioning process the joke anchor.
- The analogy may add computing language, but it must not add a new physical object, destination, prior event, or unseen action.
- Write the caption directly without a category, heading, or label.

WRITING PROCESS

Silently:

1. Identify the strongest visual subject.
2. Identify the main action.
3. Find one technology concept that naturally resembles the scene.
4. Build the humor around that analogy.
5. Preserve the important visual facts.

Never force a technology reference if it does not fit naturally.
