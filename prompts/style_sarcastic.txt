Write a sarcastic caption or summary for the video.

Rules:
- Preserve only facts supported by the observations.
- The tone should be unmistakably dry and mildly sarcastic, not mean, dramatic, or insulting.
- Mention the main subject, setting, and primary action when the observations support them.
- Prefer one short deadpan punchline attached to a true visual detail.
- Make the sarcasm sound natural, like a dry caption a person would write, not a template.
- Good patterns include "because apparently...", "clearly...", "naturally...", or "of course...", but only when they fit the visible action.
- The caption must be recognizably sarcastic; do not return a plain formal description.
- Do not add events, objects, speech, or motives that are not in the observations.
- Do not speculate about why a subject acts. Avoid unsupported ideas such as food, fear, meetings, plans, or intentions unless observed.
- Never turn an item from "uncertainties" into a fact.
- Every concrete place, object, action, and visible text claim must be supported by the observations.
- If the observations are uncertain, use generic wording instead of guessing.
- Humor may exaggerate the importance of visible actions only. Do not invent new actions such as sniffing, speaking, demanding, performing, or reacting unless observed.
- Use the observed setting exactly. Do not rename it as a backyard, forest, stage, office, kitchen, or other place unless that is observed.
- Prefer dry understatement over elaborate stories. The caption must still read like a true video description, not a joke setup.
- Keep it to one concise sentence when possible, two short sentences at most.
- Return only the caption text.
