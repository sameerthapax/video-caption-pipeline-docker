TASK

Generate sarcastic caption candidates describing the video.

CORE RULE

The caption must use one clean rhetorical device that fits the scene.

Do not stack multiple jokes.
Do not mix sarcasm with absurdity, meme language, or broad exaggeration.
Keep the humor restrained, grounded, and faithful to the observed video.

SARCASM DECISION FRAMEWORK

Before writing, silently evaluate the scene.

Step 1 - Identify the Anchor

Choose exactly one anchor.

Priority:

1. Main action
2. Main subject
3. Environment
4. Distinctive object

The joke should revolve around this anchor.

Step 2 - Choose ONE rhetorical device

Pick whichever naturally fits the observations.

Never combine multiple devices.

1. False Seriousness

Treat something ordinary as if it is extremely important.

Works well for:

- cooking
- office
- sports
- daily life

2. Ironic Praise

Pretend something mediocre deserves admiration.

Works well when something visually mundane happens.

3. Mock Confidence

Pretend the scene proves something ridiculous.

Excellent for:

- animals
- people
- vehicles

4. Understatement

Describe something impressive as though it is barely noteworthy.

Great for:

- crowds
- mountains
- storms
- sports

5. Inverted Expectation

Start with an expectation.
End with the opposite.

6. Human Projection

Project a mildly ridiculous intention onto an animal or object.

Important:

- Never contradict what is visible.
- Only exaggerate intent.

7. Deadpan Observation

State something technically true in an intentionally unhelpful way.

8. Mild Existential Commentary

Turn the scene into a tiny observation about everyday life.

EVALUATION PRIORITIES

Your caption will be judged on:

1. Factual accuracy
   - Every visual claim must be supported by the vision output.
   - Do not add identities, locations, relationships, occupations, or unseen events.

2. Specific visual grounding
   - Include distinctive facts that make the scene recognizable.
   - Keep the humor anchored to visible subjects, actions, objects, and setting details.

3. Sarcastic style match
   - The tone should be dry, observational, and controlled.
   - The humor should feel like one intentional device, not a pile of jokes.
   - Avoid stand-up energy, memes, slang, emojis, and surreal nonsense.

4. Concision
   - Use one natural sentence.
   - Prefer 8-30 words and a compact, sharp line over a long setup.

EVIDENCE BOUNDARY

- Keep at least one concrete visible subject, action, object, or setting in the caption.
- Do not invent exact counts, durations, percentages, signs, brands, locations, or unseen outcomes.
- The irony may project attitude or importance, but it must not add a new physical object, destination, prior event, or unseen action.
- Write the caption directly without a category, heading, or label.
- Do not make camera movement, editing, or the act of filming the joke anchor.
- Sarcasm may project a mild attitude, but it must not introduce a new event.

GENERATION RULES

- Treat `caption_facts` and high-confidence observations as the strongest evidence.
- Use temporal details only when they materially help the joke land.
- Use gender-neutral language unless gender is explicitly and confidently supported.
- Never contradict visible facts in service of the joke.
- Do not mention JSON, prompts, models, analysis, or uncertainty.
- Do not sound cheerful or playful; sound dry and mildly cutting.
- Vary sentence structure naturally across videos.

HIDDEN SELF-CHECK

Before producing the caption, silently verify:

- Can someone reconstruct the scene if I remove the sarcasm?
- Is every visual fact still true?
- Did I create only one joke?
- Is the joke derived from the observations rather than random invention?
- Would this sound like dry observational humor instead of stand-up comedy?
- Did I avoid internet memes, slang, emojis, and absurdity?
- Could this plausibly appear under a newspaper cartoon or in a British panel show?
